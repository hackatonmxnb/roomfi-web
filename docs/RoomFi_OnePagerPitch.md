## II. MARKET ANALYSIS & PROBLEM STATEMENT

The Protocol identifies a structural inefficiency in the Latin American housing market, characterized by a dual-sided friction that results in significant capital stagnation.

### A. The Micro-Economic Friction (User Persona)
The current market fails the mobile professional. Our analysis centers on "Maria," a representative profile of a student or professional relocating to a hub like Mexico City (CDMX). She faces:
1.  **Credit Invisibility:** Lack of cross-border credit history prevents risk assessment.
2.  **Capital Lock-up:** Requirement of up to 3 months of security deposit.
3.  **Sunk Costs:** Legal fees (~$800 USD) and lack of formal payment proofs.

## III. PROPOSED SOLUTION & ARCHITECTURE

RoomFi operates as a non-custodial technological layer that transforms static lease agreements into dynamic, liquid financial assets.

### A. Technological Pillars
*   **Hybrid Ricardian Contracts:** A legal-technological framework where the on-chain smart contract is cryptographically bound to a valid Civil Code lease. This serves as the bridge between EVM execution and court enforceability (anchored on **NOM-151** standards).
*   **Tenant Passport (Soulbound NFT):** A Zero-Knowledge Identity layer that aggregates reputational data. This creates a portable "Credit Score for Rent" that follows the user, eliminating redundant vetting.
*   **Financialization of Agreements:** By tokenizing the lease (ERC-721), The Protocol enables the use of "Future Rent" receivables as collateral, unlocking liquidity for landlords.

### B. Why Web3 Architecture?
The selection of blockchain infrastructure is not merely aesthetic but functional:
1.  **Immutability:** Replaces "trust" with "verification." Payment history is indelible.
2.  **Real-Time Auditability:** Provides lenders and landlords with instant visibility into asset performance.
3.  **Capital Efficiency:** Removes banking intermediation, allowing the "Good Payer" behavior to directly monetize their reputation through yield generation.
